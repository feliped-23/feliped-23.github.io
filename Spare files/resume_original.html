Introduction to Fusion Energy and Plasma Physics Course - Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory

During this course I learnt about the following topics:

  • • Practical, Political, and Social Dimensions - Explored the real-world challenges of fusion deployment, including regulatory risk, public perception (fusion vs. fission), and the practical strategy of replacing fossil fuel burners with fusion cores in existing infrastructure to ease adoption.
  • • Fundamentals of Fusion & Plasma Physics - Refreshed the core physical principles of fusion, plasma behavior, and key performance metrics, while deepening understanding of plasma confinement, turbulence, and instabilities that remain the central barriers to achieving stable fusion power.
  • • Fusion Devices & Engineering Challenges - Gained insight into the differences between major reactor concepts (tokamaks, spherical tokamaks, stellarators, mirror machines) and the vast engineering challenges of heating, plasma-material interactions, tritium handling, breeding blankets, and large-scale infrastructure.
  • • Complexities of Plasma Dynamics - Learned how plasmas act as fluids, oscillate as waves, and interact with turbulence, drift, and reconnection, which create the biggest obstacles in confinement and stability. This included how heating methods like neutral beam injection and microwave systems are designed to control plasma conditions.
