Capstone Project — CheckMATE

Project Summary

CheckMATE was designed to bridge the gap between online and physical chess, creating an automatically articulating chessboard that preserves the look and feel of a traditional set. The system integrates mechanical, electrical, and software subsystems into a seamless playing experience.

  • A sensing layer detects piece positions using custom hall-effect sensors and NFC tags.
  • An H-Bot gantry system moves game pieces across the board at high speed (up to 450 mm/s) with smooth accelerations.
  • A 20 kg-force electromagnet engages with embedded neodymium magnets in the chess pieces, allowing reliable capture and movement.
  • A Raspberry Pi with touchscreen interface runs the chess engine, displays legal moves, and manages user interaction.
  • Custom electronics, power distribution, and firmware ensure efficient operation while maintaining safety and minimal heat buildup.

Together, these elements create a board that can autonomously execute moves, handle special chess maneuvers like castling and knight jumps, and support both local and online gameplay.

My Contributions

My role focused on ensuring strong and reliable magnetic coupling and smooth piece movement, as well as contributing to the overall mechanical and control systems:

  • Electromagnet Selection & Testing
    • Evaluated and tested electromagnets of different sizes and strengths.
    • Experimented with various permanent magnets embedded in the pieces to maximize coupling force while minimizing interference between pieces.
    • Optimized the board thickness to balance magnetic performance with a low-friction playing surface.
  • Surface and Board Design
    • Designed and fabricated the playing surface with minimal width and friction to ensure reliable sliding of pieces.
    • Integrated laser-etched markings and epoxy finishing for durability and precision.
  • Mechanical Gantry & Frame Construction
    • Played a key role in the H-Bot gantry system design and build, implementing early-stage stepper motor control via Universal G-code Sender on the prototype.
    • Led the construction of the frame, case, and housing, ensuring a rigid, precise, and accessible structure for the system.
    • Fabricated chess pieces and mechanical components through additive manufacturing (3D printing).
  • Software & Control
    • Helped implement GRBL as the G-code interpreter to interface motion control with the chess logic.
    • Contributed to coding the gantry movement logic, including handling special moves and reorganizing the board during setup or captures.
