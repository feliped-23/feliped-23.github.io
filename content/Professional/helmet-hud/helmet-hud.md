# Helmet Heads-Up Display — Near-Eye AR Display for Formula Electric

**Type:** Project (3rd Year Capstone)
**Period:** Sep 2022 – Apr 2023
**Organization:** University of British Columbia — IGEN 330 Intermediate Engineering Design

## Summary

Constructed a helmet-mounted, augmented-reality heads-up display (HUD) for an electric racing kart, projecting real-time CAN BUS telemetry into the driver's field of view. Engineered the near-eye display (NED) module, conducted exhaustive optical physics research on focal lengths, virtual image placement, and combiner geometries, then fabricated the full opto-mechanical assembly including the optical combiner, lensing system, and a custom NED casing tailored to helmet ergonomics.

## Key Achievements

- Virtual image distance increased by over 2 orders of magnitude via custom bi-convex lens system (~300 mm virtual image vs. naked OLED)
- Compact helmet-mounted form factor with magnetic Quick Disconnect System (QDS) compliant with Formula SAE safety standards
- CAN BUS data capture with minimal latency to driver display
- Empirically validated lens configuration with multiple test users; confirmed improved clarity and reduced eye strain vs. naked OLED
- Chose OLED + bi-convex lens over direct projection to reduce driver eye accommodation strain at speed
- Iteratively benchmarked multiple beam-splitter materials and coatings; minimized ghosting and reflection artifacts before finalizing
- Sealed optical housing designed to resist moisture and debris in racing environments
- Full report and project poster available

## Tools & Tech

SolidWorks, optical combiners, bi-convex lensing, beam-splitter, OLED micro-display, CAN BUS, FDM 3D printing, custom optical test rig, electronics integration
