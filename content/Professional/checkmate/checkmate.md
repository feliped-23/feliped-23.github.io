# CheckMATE — Autonomous Robotic Chessboard

**Type:** Project (4th Year Capstone)
**Period:** Sep 2024 – Apr 2025
**Organization:** University of British Columbia — IGEN 430 Advanced Engineering Design

## Summary

Led a cross-functional senior capstone team to design and build CheckMATE, a robotic chessboard that merges traditional gameplay with automated, computer-driven piece movement. Owned project coordination across mechanical, electrical, and software workstreams — sequencing design reviews, sub-team integrations, and testing milestones to deliver a consumer-ready prototype within a single academic year. The project was voted Best Capstone Project by UBC faculty, peers, and public showcase attendees.

## Key Achievements

- H-Bot gantry achieving 450 mm/s top speed and 600 mm/s² acceleration for smooth, fast piece motion
- 20 kg-force electromagnet coupling enabling reliable piece actuation and capture without mechanical grippers
- Handles all special chess maneuvers autonomously: castling, knight jumps, piece capture with path rerouting
- Designed 3 mm-thick low-friction epoxy playing surface — maintains traditional board aesthetics over the gantry
- Applied GD&T tolerancing to bearing mounts and electromagnet carriage on FDM-printed housings
- Chose electromagnet + embedded neodymium approach over mechanical grippers; preserved traditional aesthetics while enabling capturer rerouting
- Awarded Best Capstone Project at DAID (Design and Innovation Day) showcase
- Full report, poster, and demo video available

## Tools & Tech

SolidWorks, H-Bot gantry, stepper motors, Universal G-code Sender (GRBL), electromagnets, neodymium magnets, Raspberry Pi, FDM 3D printing, electromechanical integration, GD&T
