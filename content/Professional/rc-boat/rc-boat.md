# Autonomous Water-Testing RC Boat — Industrial Water Quality Monitoring

**Type:** Project (2nd Year Capstone)
**Period:** Dec 2021 – Apr 2022
**Organization:** University of British Columbia — IGEN 230 Introduction to Engineering Design

## Summary

Designed an autonomous catamaran hull and electromechanical system for remote water quality monitoring in industrial tailings ponds, eliminating the need for manual sampling in hazardous environments. Spearheaded fiberglass hull manufacturing using a 3D-printed negative mold, executing layup, curing, and finishing to achieve a watertight composite shell, then assembled the full propulsion, steering, sensor, and electronics system into the hull. Awarded Best 2nd-Year Capstone Project by UBC.

## Key Achievements

- 80 × 56 × 21 cm fiberglass catamaran hull optimized for stability and payload capacity
- Sensor winch capable of sampling to 50 m depth via stepper-driven assembly
- Full autonomous navigation with GPS + IMU + gyro integration via flight-controller stack
- Selected twin-hull catamaran geometry over monohull; validated through SolidWorks buoyancy and stability calculations
- Used multi-part 3D-printed negative mold for fiberglass lay-up — enabled complex geometry without CNC tooling
- Applied vacuum-bag seal during curing to improve laminate density and structural rigidity
- Passed leak-testing, flotation trials, and controlled water-tank validation before full-system integration
- Eliminates manual sampling risk in hazardous industrial tailings pond environments
- Full report, poster, and individual logbook available

## Tools & Tech

SolidWorks, FDM 3D printing (mold), fiberglass / composite layup, vacuum bagging, RC electronics, GPS + IMU flight controller, stepper-driven winch, sensor integration, Li-Po power systems
