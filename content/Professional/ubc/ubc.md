# BASc, Integrated Engineering — University of British Columbia

**Type:** Education
**Period:** 2020 – May 2026
**Organization:** University of British Columbia — Vancouver, BC

## Summary

Cross-disciplinary BASc combining mechanical, electrical, and electromagnetic engineering through UBC's Integrated Engineering program. The program is structured around a design-thread capstone sequence (IGEN 230 / 330 / 430) running across 2nd, 3rd, and 4th year — three year-long, team-based, open-ended design projects each producing a working prototype. Graduating with a Mechanical major and Electrical / Electromagnetic minor.

## Technical Focus Areas

**Mechanical core:** Mechanics of Materials (MECH 260/360), Heat Transfer (MECH 375), Machine Design (MECH 325), Engineering Materials (APSC 278/279), Materials in Design (MTRL 280), Engineering Economics (MECH 431), Materials Handling (MINE 465)

**Electrical / electromagnetic core:** Linear Circuits (ELEC 204), Electronics Lab (ELEC 205), Systems and Control (ELEC 341), Applied Electronics & Electromechanics (ELEC 344), Engineering Electromagnetics (ELEC 211), Electromagnetic Fields and Waves (ELEC 311), Digital Systems and Microcomputers (CPEN 312), Vector Calculus for EE (MATH 264)

**Electives:** Engineering Electromagnetics I & II, Machine Design, FEA/FEM (ANSYS), Computer Vision & Image Processing (via University of Sydney exchange)

## Key Achievements

- Best 2nd-Year Capstone Project — IGEN 230 (Autonomous RC Boat, 2022)
- Three year-long design capstones spanning robotics, AR optics, and autonomous vehicles
