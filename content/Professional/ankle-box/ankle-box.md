# Ankle Box — Variable-Resistance Desk Footrest

**Type:** Personal Design Project
**Period:** Nov 2025 – Present
**Organization:** Self-initiated — Vancouver, BC

## Summary

Self-initiated mechanical design project engineering a desk-mounted footrest that provides 2-axis, bidirectional resistance for ankle strength and rehabilitation training across three independently controlled surfaces. Designed a variable-resistance pneumatic subsystem with a custom gearing and selective clutching assembly that distributes force from a single actuator to each surface, enabling tunable resistance. Optimized entirely for additive manufacturing with DFM principles, enabling tool-free assembly by non-technical end users. Mastered OnShape CAD from scratch over the course of the project.

## Key Achievements

- 3 independently controlled resistance surfaces from a single pneumatic rotary actuator
- Custom gearing + selective clutching assembly enables tunable resistance distribution without multiple actuators
- 4+ iterative printed revisions on critical load paths (gearing, clutch, shaft coupling)
- 2-axis bidirectional resistance for ankle strength and rehabilitation training
- Optimized for FDM manufacturing using DFM/DFA principles — tool-free assembly for non-technical end users
- Applied GD&T to press-fit and tight-coupling interfaces (shaft couplings, clutch bores, bearing seats) accounting for FDM layer orientation, material shrinkage, and printer variability
- Stress-tested critical components under expected loading: gearing assembly, rotary actuator, shaft coupling
- Self-initiated and self-funded from concept to working prototype
- Translated a real user need (in-office ankle rehab and strengthening) into mechanical requirements end-to-end

## Tools & Tech

OnShape, FDM 3D printing, pneumatic rotary actuator, custom gearing, selective clutching, GD&T, DFM/DFA, rapid prototyping, stress testing
